The Chapters of K.V.T

My name is Kier VaSean Turpin, and every chapter of my life has carried a different name, a different energy, and a different purpose.

Back when I played basketball in the military, people knew me as Penny. That name came from the court — from the grind, the discipline, the competition, and the brotherhood that shaped me during those years. It was a chapter built on physical strength, teamwork, and pushing myself past limits.

While I was still in the military, another part of me started to grow: dance. Movement became expression, release, and identity. When I got out, dance became my world. Teaching, performing, connecting — that era taught me how to lead, how to communicate without words, and how to bring people together through rhythm and creativity.

Later, when I stepped into gaming and streaming, a new name emerged: Meahgee. That identity lived online — in the games I played, the communities I built, and the early days of me learning code. Meahgee was the bridge between who I had been and who I was becoming. It was the era where I started exploring technology, creativity, and digital identity all at once.

Today, I’m stepping fully into myself as Kier, and building my future under the name K.V.T.

Not just initials — but an acronym that represents my mission:
Knowledge. Versatility. Tech.
Everything I create, release, or build for the world will live under that name.

I recently earned my Bachelor’s in Information Technology from the University of Arizona Global Campus, and I’m preparing to walk the stage at commencement. I now serve as the Alumni Relations Officer for the UAGC Student Veterans of America chapter, where I help strengthen connections between graduates, support veteran students, and build a network rooted in mentorship and belonging.

I’m the founder of K.V.Tech and the creator of MoodMix, an emotional-wellness music app designed to help people understand and express what they’re feeling through sound, color, and intuitive design. MoodMix grew out of my own experiences navigating emotion, identity, and resilience — and from my belief that technology should feel human, supportive, and accessible.

My creative identity is shaped by my heritage and history. I carry that with me in everything I build. The colors I choose, the symbols I use, the stories I tell — they all come from a place of reclaiming knowledge, honoring ancestry, and pushing back against the erasure that has followed Black people throughout history. Even my logo reflects that: Black for Knowledge, because we were denied it, yet we’ve contributed more to the world than history books ever admit.

Outside of tech, I’m passionate about broadcasting and live production. I’ve spent time inside newsrooms and control rooms, learning how stories move from idea to screen. I produce music and I’m drawn to environments where creativity, precision, and timing come together like playing DnD ( Dungeons and Dragons ) table top game. All of these things and others possess the same energy that drives my work in tech.

Right now, I’m focused on advocacy. I’m committed to improving systems, uplifting communities, and creating tools that make life better for people who are often overlooked. Whether it’s through technology, mentorship, storytelling, or design, I want my work to open doors, not close them.

Every chapter — Penny, the dancer, Meahgee, Kier, and now K.V.T. — has shaped the person I am today.

"And everything I build is rooted in one mission:
Create with purpose. Lead with empathy. Leave things better than I found them."
